Aerocity, the bridge between real world infrastructure and decentralization

Global integration through technology will become the key factor for the Future and therefore we are looking to create a smart Urban Hub City incorporating key elements, which will encompass utilization of technology to secure the lifestyle, health and well-being of its inhabitants. The Pilot project of the South Bucharest Aerocity will provide a proof of concept of a global plan for future city development. Romania has been selected as the existing infrastructure requires substantial investment, it has a strong IT and language educated population, is part of the EU but has lower barriers to entry for new ideas and concepts.

By 2050 the world's population is expected to reach 9.8 billion. Nearly 70 percent of this booming population—6.7 billion people— is projected to live in urban areas. We asked our architectural and urban planning experts to design the city of the future.

The hey principles of AeroCity'S design

We have configured a new approach to allow people to be more empowered to live meaningful and fulfilled lives, enabled seamlessly by technology, offering exciting opportunities for all. Developments in digital technology present opportunities to enhance our strengths, overcome national challenges and physical limits, and build new sources of comparative advantage. Sustainability and environmental protection are a key design parameter in this project. We will achieve a fully 'green; AeroCity through the development of renewable energy sources across the project. These will be provided to all areas of the project; we will also preserve the energy created through conversion and reutilisation of heat and carbon waste in other areas of AeroCity. We will develop a hydrogen production plant and distribution outlets. The non-recyclable waste materials will be used in a 'waste to energy' incinerator to facilitate a zero-landfill solution.
